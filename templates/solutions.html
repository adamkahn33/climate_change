Solutions:

"In executing its mission, NASA's sustainability objectives are to:

1. increase energy efficiency;
2. increase the use of renewable energy;
3. measure, report, and reduce NASA's direct and indirect greenhouse gas emissions;
4. conserve and protect water resources through efficiency, reuse, and stormwater management;
5. eliminate waste, prevent pollution, and increase recycling;
6. leverage agency acquisitions to foster markets for sustainable technologies and environmentally preferable materials, products, and services;
7. design, construct, maintain, and operate high-performance sustainable buildings;
8. utilize power management options and reduce the number of agency data centers;
9. support economic growth and livability of the communities where NASA conducts business;
10. evaluate agency climate change risks and vulnerabilities and develop mitigation and adaptation measures to manage both the short-and long-term effects of climate change on the agency's mission and operations;
11. raise employee awareness and encourage each individual in the NASA community to apply the concepts of sustainability to every aspect of their daily work to achieve these goals;
12. maintain compliance with all applicable federal, state, local or territorial law and regulations related to energy security, a healthy environment, and environmentally-sound operations; and
13. comply with internal NASA requirements and agreements with other entities."
