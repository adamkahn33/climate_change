Effects:

Some of the long-term effects of climate change found within the United States are as follows: temperatures will continues to rise, the frost-free/growing seasons will lengethen, drastic/radical changes in weather patterns including more droughts, heat waves, and percipitation. Hurricanes will become stronger and sea levels will rise as ice in the arctic continues to melt.

"The current warming trend is of particular significance because most of it is extremely likely (greater than 95% probability) to be the result of human activity since the mid-20th century and proceeding at a rate that is unprecedented over decades to millennia.1" IPCC Fifth Assessment Report, Summary for Policymakers
